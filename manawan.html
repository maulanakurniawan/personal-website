Independent Initiative

Manawan

Manawan is my independent product initiative. It’s where I build small, useful software tools with a practical mindset: solve a specific problem well, keep things simple, and improve steadily based on real usage.

Why this initiative exists

I’ve spent years building software for different contexts, and one thing stays true: many teams and individuals need focused tools more than bloated platforms. Manawan gives me a dedicated space to create products that are clear in purpose, reliable in daily use, and realistic in scope.

This initiative supports my work as a developer; it doesn’t replace it. The products I build here sharpen my engineering judgment and keep me close to real-world user needs.

Current products

WebhookWatch

WebhookWatch helps monitor webhook events and delivery behavior so integration failures can be detected and fixed quickly. It is built for developers who need better visibility into webhook pipelines.

SoloHours

SoloHours is a straightforward time tracking tool for freelancers. The product is designed to make daily tracking easier and provide cleaner records for planning and billing.

Future products

Manawan is intentionally long-term. New tools will be added as I find practical problems worth solving and can support them with the quality users deserve.

Product philosophy

I prefer focused products with clear boundaries. I avoid unnecessary complexity, prioritize operational reliability, and aim for thoughtful UX that respects user time. This philosophy comes from years of backend work, production maintenance, and seeing what actually holds up in the long run.
